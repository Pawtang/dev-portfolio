D E S K L A M P/PROJECT

Ideation

I began with some very simple concept designs on isometric paper. I don't like to start with specific dimensions or really even specific goals in mind, but isometrics help get a consistent idea of scale and use of 3-dimensional space.

My first step is always just brainstorming; seeing whatever comes out. In this case, I didn't even end up following one of my sketches, but they started to inform what type of materials I wanted to use and how I wanted my light to work in its environment.

Iteration

I find that when building something with new methods, I tend to stray a bit from the plan I go in with as I learn the new medium and tools. Building the curve of the wood, attaching it to the base, and running the wire all turned out to be challenges that shaped the final structure.

Counterbored wood screws through the base for attachment

Finished form without any electronics

Soldering LEDs into inner curve

Final Product

After a lot of soldering and resoldering, wire splicing and unsplicing, and a coat of linseed oil, the lamp was finished. I spliced a touch-fader switch into the power cord, allowing you to control the brightness of the LEDs; a huge asset for a desk lamp. I can have it at full brightness for reading, drawing, or doing any detail-oriented work. Or I can have it at low power and just enjoy a low glow.
